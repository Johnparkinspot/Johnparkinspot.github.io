Example of a Monalphabetic Cipher

Monalphabetic Ciphers encode each unique Plaintext to a unique cipher text. No two different letters get encoded to a single letter. Given this cipher, the best way to go about solving it is first by using frequency analysis. Since E is statistically the most common plaintext, we can try to find the most frequently occuring ciphertext replace it with E. We can also replace 3 letter words with common trigraphs to further help us decode the cipher.
